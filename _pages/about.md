I am **Heng Wei (魏珩)**, currently pursuing a Ph.D. degree at Nanjing Forestry University.

I am passionate about exploring the macro-scale evolution of the natural world and the feedback mechanisms of the Earth system. Against the backdrop of global climate change, understanding how terrestrial ecosystems respond to and adapt under environmental disturbances is not only a central scientific challenge, but also an important question for humanity's future.

My research mainly focuses on **global change and ecosystem carbon sinks**. I work on global climate change, the dynamic evolution of carbon sources and sinks in terrestrial ecosystems, and regional environmental security.

Through an interdisciplinary perspective, I hope to decode the underlying response logic of ecosystems under complex environmental disturbances more precisely, thereby providing theoretical support for assessing carbon sink potential, improving ecosystem resilience, and addressing global climate change.

## Research Interests

- Global climate change
- Terrestrial ecosystem carbon source-sink dynamics
- Ecosystem carbon sinks
- Regional environmental security
- Ecosystem responses and resilience under environmental disturbance

My published work covers cropland carbon sinks, karst vegetation carbon sink change, phosphorus limitation in karst regions, and soil conservation evaluation. See the full list on the [Publications page]({{ "/publications/" | relative_url }}).
